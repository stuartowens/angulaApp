ARE YOU READY

TO PLAY IN A BAND?

JOIN OUR CAMP in the beautiful hills of North Carolina and let us help you move from jamming to an organized performance!

"I Want to be in a Band" Camp is open to singers and acoustic musicians interested in performing solo or as duos, trios, and full bands. Already-formed bands are also welcome.

  • All instrutors are seasoned music professionals.

  • Prerequisite

    Must have skill level with instrument or voice to feel comfortable performing in front of a live audience.

  • Musicians under 16 welcome with accompanying chaperone.

I WANT TO BE IN A BAND CAMP IS AN immersion experience WHERE ALL PARTICIPANT ARE GROUPED INTO BANDS AND ARE COACHED PM TOPICS INCLUDING:

  • ensemble playing
  • arrangments
  • lead vocals & harmony vocals
  • breaks & fills
  • live playing
  • stage presence
  • understanding & setting up a PA
  • playing over a PA
  • MC work
  • songwriting
  • booking
  • management
  • band member personal relationship
  • publishing
  • travel & transportaion
  • recording
  • producing
  • marketing & social media
  • and more

NEWS

How do we define “success” in the bluegrass music business? We certainly can’t do it by the standards used in the mass market music world, where success is considered to have been achieved when you own your own airplane and can no longer appear in public places. In other words, you’re a success when your lifestyle has become lavish but almost impossible to manage, at least without an entourage, which is also almost impossible to manage. We aim a little lower in our world. As a general rule, if you’re playing bluegrass music for “a living” and your income is near or above the poverty line, you’ve pretty much made it.

Chris Jones
